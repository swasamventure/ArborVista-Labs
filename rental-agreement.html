Short-Term Guest Rental Agreement

Arbor Vista Retreat · Sevierville, Tennessee

Testing draft: This web version is adapted from the supplied template and should be reviewed by a Tennessee attorney before production use. The signature fields used for the Book Direct flow are available on the Book Direct page.
  1. Agreement and reservation. This agreement supplements the reservation terms accepted through the applicable booking channel. The booking-channel terms govern in the event of a conflict.
  2. Payment and cancellation. Payment timing and cancellation terms will be stated in the approved reservation confirmation. No payment is collected by this test website.
  3. Occupancy. The standard sleeping setup accommodates six guests. Occupancy of seven or eight guests is permitted only with prior written Host approval and confirmed sofa-bed availability. Overnight occupancy may not exceed the guest count approved for the reservation.
  4. Property use. The cabin may be used only for lawful residential vacation purposes. Parties, events and commercial activity are prohibited.
  5. No smoking or vaping. Smoking or vaping is prohibited inside the cabin and near entrances. The fee may be up to $2,000 plus restoration costs.
  6. Pets. Pets are not permitted unless approved in writing. Unauthorized pets may result in a $500 fee plus cleaning or restoration costs. Service-animal requests will be handled as required by applicable law.
  7. Check-in and checkout. Check-in begins at 4:00 PM. Checkout is by 10:00 AM. Unauthorized late checkout may be charged at $100 per hour.
  8. Condition and reporting. Guests should report cleanliness, maintenance or damage concerns promptly after arrival so the Host can respond.
  9. Damage and extraordinary cleaning. Guests are responsible for damage, missing items and extraordinary cleaning caused by their group, visitors or invitees.
  10. House and resort rules. Guests must follow Arbor Vista Retreat and Shagbark Resort rules, including gate registration requirements.
  11. Wildlife and amenities. Use of the hot tub, pool, decks, fireplaces and other amenities is at the guest’s own risk. Food and scented items must be secured to reduce bear activity.
  12. Personal property. The Host is not responsible for loss or damage to guest vehicles or personal belongings except as required by law.
  13. Utilities and interruptions. The Host will make reasonable efforts to address service interruptions but cannot guarantee uninterrupted utilities, internet, weather or resort services.
  14. Disputes. The parties will first attempt to resolve disputes through direct discussion. Any court proceeding will occur in the county and state where the accommodation is located, subject to applicable law.
  15. Electronic acceptance. Electronic acceptance and signatures may be treated as originals to the extent permitted by law.
